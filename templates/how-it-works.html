How It Works

Understanding the science behind audio deepfake detection

1. Audio Upload

You upload your audio file (.wav format) through our secure interface. The file is temporarily processed in memory without being stored on our servers.

2. Feature Extraction

Our system extracts Mel-Spectrogram features from the audio, converting sound waves into visual representations that reveal hidden patterns invisible to the human ear.

3. AI Analysis

The extracted features are fed into our trained neural network model, which analyzes patterns and artifacts that distinguish real audio from synthetic deepfakes.

4. Result Generation

The model outputs a confidence score and classification, determining whether the audio is "Real" or "Fake" based on learned patterns from thousands of training samples.

Technical Deep Dive

Mel-Spectrogram Analysis

We convert audio signals into Mel-Spectrograms, which represent the frequency content of audio over time. This visual representation reveals subtle artifacts left by deepfake generation algorithms.

  • Frequency domain analysis
  • Time-frequency representation
  • Perceptual scaling

Neural Network Architecture

Our detection model uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) specifically designed for audio analysis, trained on diverse datasets of real and synthetic audio.

  • Convolutional layers for pattern recognition
  • Pooling layers for feature reduction
  • Dense layers for classification

Artifact Detection

Deepfake audio often contains subtle artifacts that our model is trained to identify, including spectral inconsistencies and unnatural frequency patterns.

  • Spectral artifacts
  • Phase inconsistencies
  • Temporal anomalies

Robustness Features

Our system is designed to handle various audio qualities and formats while maintaining high accuracy across different types of deepfake generation methods.

  • Noise robustness
  • Compression tolerance
  • Multi-generator detection
